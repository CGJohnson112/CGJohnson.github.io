About Me

Why this field?/How I got started

The first language I learned was BASIC, through a BASICTutor computer back in the late 80s. At about 2004, I took an HTML/CSS class at Washburn University, along with Adobe Illustrator, PhotoShop, Indesign, and Flash, basic photography, business and technical writing. These skills were put toward building my DJ website.

Most of the site's dynamics on djcapricejohnson.com was non-existent, except for an image animation page on the landing area, surrounded by an elegant picture frame (I was showing off! This was also efore JavaScript was invented). After the invention of JS and when my Flash software stopped working, I learned how to do the image carousel using a script file. The results were pretty cool, so I wanted to learn more on writing code. Tutorials and books started coming out on the subject, so I found some of the best written ones, like "Javascript & Jquery" By Jon Duckett. Video tutorials also supplemented much of my education, until CodeCademy and FreeCodeCamp were a thing on the internet.

My site later went from spaghetti-coded HTML/CSS/JS/jQuery to spaghetti-coded PHP. Now, SuburbanStyleProductions is coded to Laravel, using MVC and compliation of code to make a much better built site.

Most recently, I picked up learning on frameworks like Laravel and Angular CLI, while learning other nuances like ES6, constructors, changes to languages with new object tages such as const, let and a host of others.

I mostly take template sites and convert them into the frameworks to xreate functionality to them through both front-end and back-end technology. Some of my challenges include working with JSON/REST APIs to dsiplay key-value pairs on a webpage, MySQL/phpMyAdmin, making applications that users can use with ease and that look good, and take advantage of the best they can provide for their customers and end users.
